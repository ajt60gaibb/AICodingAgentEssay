• Implemented a minimal Python package for the discretized BVP and Newton solve with backtracking line
  search.

  The solver is in nonlinear_bvp/solver.py. It uses the second-order finite-difference residual
  [
  F_i(u)=\frac{-u_{i-1}+2u_i-u_{i+1}}{h^2}+u_i^3-1,\quad h=\frac1{N+1},\ N=200,
  ]
  with u_0 = u_{N+1} = 0, and the corresponding tridiagonal Jacobian. Newton steps are computed with
  np.linalg.solve, and an Armijo backtracking line search is applied to 0.5 * ||F(u)||^2.

  Tests are in tests/test_solver.py. They verify:

  - residual/Jacobian dimensions and stencil structure,
  - convergence of the Newton solver for N=200,
  - quadratic local convergence near the solution by checking ||e_{k+1}|| / ||e_k||^2 stays bounded over two
    full Newton steps.

  Project config is in pyproject.toml. Verification: pytest -q passes with 3 passed in 0.10s.
